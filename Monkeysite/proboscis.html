Proboscis

Probiscis monkeys are old-world monkeys, commonly known for their long noses.

Overview

The proboscis monkey is a long-tailed arboreal primate native to the island of Borneo (in Southeast Asia). They have partially webbed hands and feet, an unusual adaptation among primates, which helps them swim.

Fun Facts!

  • Proboscis monkeys get their name from the word "Probiscis" meaning "the nose of a mammal, especially when it is long and mobile, such as the trunk of an elephant".
  • They are only native to the previously mentioned island, Borneo.
  • They possess 9 different stomachs, making htem known as the cows of the primate world.

Habitat

Proboscis monkeys are only native to the jungles of Borneo, usually being around the rivers, coastal mangroves and swamps. They are highly aboreal and rarely venture onto land, where the forage for food.

Diet

Similarly to most species of monkeys, proboscis' mostly eat leaves and fruits that they find throught the forests that they inahabit, although they can also be found to eat foods such as flowers, tree bark, insects and larvae. And on rare occasisions they have been observed eating crabs and mushrooms.

Behaviour

They live in groups known as "harems" usually consisting of one dominant male and 2 to 7 females, aswell as their offspring. Although many of these groups end up congregating together by river side to sleep at night.
