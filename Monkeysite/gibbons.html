Gibbons

Gibbons are most commonly found in the tropical and subtropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

Overview

Gibbons are acrobatic primates, that primarily reside in trees. They can be easily characterised by their long arms and legs that alolow them to easily swing through trees. One of the most defining characteristics of gibbons is their lack of tails. Gibbons are very socialable, in comparison to other primates, as they form lots of bonds and occasionaly will mate for life.

Fun Facts!

  • Gibbons are able to swing as far as 15 meters in one swing.
  • Gibbons are monogamous creatures, and live in what we would call a nuclear family.
  • Their scientific name is "Hylobatidae"

Habitat

Native gibbon habitats are found in trainforests in the East, South and Southeast of asia. They can be found in countries such as China, Thailand, Cambodia, Laos Vietnam, Bangladesh, India, Malatsia and Indonesia. They spend most of their time in the upper and middle levels of trees canopy. They do spend time on the ground foraging for food however.

Diet

Gibbons are omnivorous, they eat fruits, leaves, nuts and some insects. They are especially fond of eating figs

Behaviour

Gibbons are very friendly around humans, and may approach them to climb them. They can sometimes be found walking around on the ground, and will commonly raise their arms above to balance themselves.
